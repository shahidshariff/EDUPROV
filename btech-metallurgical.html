B.Tech Metallurgical Engineering

Course

Metallurgy Engineering is a 4 year professional full-time degree offered by engineering colleges. Metallurgy engineering covers a wide spectrum of subjects like Physical metallurgy, Extraction metallurgy, Mineral processing, thermodynamics, fluid flow, mass and heat transport, chemical kinetics etc. Basic subjects including Advanced PCM (physics, chemistry, mathematics) and other basic engineering subjects form the curriculum for the first year followed by technical subjects in the subsequent years. After the end of four years the candidate is conferred with degree of B.Tech (Bachelor of technology) or B.E. (Bachelor of Engineering) in Metallurgy.

Scope

The scope ofMetallurgy Engineering is very diverse and offers lucrative job prospects for young engineers. There are some exciting job opportunities in private as well as in govt sector. You can also opt for a Research Fellow programmer or go for a teaching profession by procuring a Masters degree. Metallurgy engineers also offered jobs in aviation sector, automobile sector, micro electronics, shipping, metal casting etc. In almost every field where metals are produced, extracted, manufactured, refined, purchased or sold, there is a demand of a metallurgy engineer. You can also opt for higher studies (Masters or Doctorate degree) in any branch related to metallurgy.

There is a high-pitched rise in demand of Metallurgical Engineers in abroad. You can apply for the jobs anytime or you can also go for Research in the foreign universities.

The salary increases with skills and years of experience. Core Companies like Hindalco, Nalco, Steel companies recruit metallurgy engineers at very lucrative packages. Even Oil companies and Automobile sector also hold good demand of metallurgy engineer. You can anytime switch to other sector like IT, banking etc based on your skills.
