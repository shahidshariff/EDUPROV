B.Tech Production Engineering

Course

It is a blend of manufacturing, engineering technologies, and management. The main aim role of a production engineer is to develop efficient processes related to turning raw materials to finished products. An aspirant has a wide knowledge of engineering practices and has awareness about production-related management challenges. Production engineers are responsible for the production process. This branch of engineering overlaps certain other courses such as manufacturing engineering and industrial engineering. The scope of the course is high and the one who chooses this course can shine in the country and outside the country too.

Bachelor of Technology (B. Tech.) or Bachelor of Engineering (B.E.) in productionis an undergraduate course pursued in an engineering college for a duration of four years. During the first year, of course, students deal with the fundamental subjects like Mathematics, Physics, and chemistry, Engineering materials, thermodynamics, mechanics, industrial statistics, etc followed by core technical subjects such as Project Planning, Manufacturing Plastics, and ceramics, terotechnology, ergonomics, Non- Destructive Testing, quality circle, and work motivation, etc. After completing this course successfully, the student is conferred B.Tech/B.E. in Production Engineering as mostly referred to as a production engineer

Eligibility

  • The eligibility criteria comprise qualifying 10+2 with science subjects essentially Physics, chemistry, and mathematics.

There are two main nationwide competitive entrance exams conducted. For taking admissions into IITs, one needs to qualify in JEE (Joint Entrance Exam). For NITs, AIEEE (All India Engineering entrance exam) should be cleared with a good rank. Some state colleges take the merit of AIEEE, while some take their own exam.

Scope

The scope of this engineering is very versatile as aproduction engineer work in a similar manner as an industrial engineer and a little bit of a mechanical engineer. Production engineers are responsible for creating/ designing the most innovative and efficient processes for the effective use of machines and materials. They have designated the roles of Quality Manager, Quality tester, Manager (production), Assistant manager, marketing officer, and analyst, etc. Production engineers are also responsible for striking a balance between the production unit and the supply unit.

One can also get into Software Company or consultancy with his excellent coding and analytical skills. One can also opt for the teaching profession by pursuingM.E. / M. Tech in production engineering. Production engineers enjoy more demand in foreign countries. They are offered very lucrative packages and incentives over there.
