B.Sc Pure Science

Pure science is one of the most important courses as we require it in our day to day life and it builds and improves our knowledge. Students can opt subjects related to physical sciences, mathematical science and natural sciences. Students who took up science streams in 11th and 12th require career advice and career counselling, mainly because they have so many options to choose from. I’m sure the students are not sure what subjects to take up, what stream is the best, which college is the best for their course etc. So, career counselling for science students helps them narrow down choices and choose the best college for taking admission there.

Pure science can mainly be divided into physical science, mathematical science and natural science.

The best colleges and universities like IITs, NITs, IISc (Indian Institute of Science), IISERs (Indian Institute of Scientific Education and research), BITS, NISER etc. offers integrated BS-MS degrees in pure science. Students will have to take up entrance exams for getting admission in these colleges. Here students finish both their undergraduate and master’s degrees in 5 years. The career options they have are discussed below. So, keep reading to know more about it.

Entrance Exams

  • IIT JEE Mains
  • IIT JEE Advance
  • BITS online exam
  • NEST
  • KVPY

IITs and NITs allow student admission with IIT JEE ranks only. Whereas IISc Bangalore and IISERs have student admissions based on their IIT JEE ranks and KVPY ranks. BITS on the other hand has its own online entrance exam that happens all over the country. NISER also has an entrance exam called NEST it is conducted in Bangalore once a year during the month of June.

Scope

Jobs after BSc here a few BSc career opportunities that can be achieved. These include jobs in private sector and the government jobs also.

The job options after a BSc in physical science include jobs like geophysicists, higher education lecturer, metallurgist, research scientist, seismologist, radiation expert, school teacher,physicists, engineering professionals, project developers, finance and investment analysts etc. They can be employed by companies and institutions dealing with aerospace, defence, energy, engineering, manufacturing, instrumentation, oil and gas, science, telecommunication and manufacturing.

Students had mathematics as a subject can take up jobs after BSc like researcher, statistician, professor, aerospace engineer, chartered accountant if they do CA after BSc, investment banker, meteorologist, surveyor, banking jobs, software testers, civil service, computing etc.

Those students who studied chemistry in college get offers from industries that deal with chemicals, pharmaceuticals, agrochemicals, plastics, polymers, food and drinks, utilities and research, health, medical organisations, scientific research organisation etc. They can take up jobs after BSc like toxicologist, research scientist in biology and physical sciences, pharmacologist, forensic scientist, health care scientist, clinical biochemist, lecturer, teacher and even government jobs.

Students of BSc in biology also have a wide range of opportunities. Right after BSc they can clear entrance exams to get a job in forest department, environment science etc. They can become molecular biologists, microbiologists, pharmacologists, research scientists, medical scientist, wildlife and nature conservationists, science writers etc. biology students can take up MSc in Dietetics, nutrition and food science to become doctors of nutrition and dietitians. These are a high demand jobs in all the top hospitals, healthcare services and institutions.
