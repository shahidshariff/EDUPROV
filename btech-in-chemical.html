B.Tech Chemical Engineering

Chemical Engineering is the branch of life science. Chemical engineering involves designing and maintaining chemical plants and the development of raw materials through chemical processing. It combines the knowledge of chemistry, biochemistry, biology, microbiology, mathematics, and physics along with engineering for the production of chemicals and by its products.

The Chemical Engineering applied in manufacturing wide variety products such as fibres, fabrics, paints, medical drugs, biomaterials, gasoline, lubricants etc used in various industries such as textile, food, plastics, automotive, aerospace, petroleum, oil and gas, biomedical, biotechnology and pharmaceuticals, thereby increasing the scope of Chemical Engineering. organic, inorganic, Agrochemicals (herbicides, insecticides, and fertilizers), petroleum, industrial chemical, ceramics, elastomers, explosives, detergent products, plastics, dietary supplements, pharmaceuticals, fragrance, and flavors. Chemical engineers design, maintain and operate large scale machinery, industrial plants and chemical processes required for the production of various usable products as mentioned above. Therefore, this field is not just related to chemistry. In order to design the equipment's and scale up the products from lab to industrial scale manufacturing, you will be studying a lot of concepts in physics, mathematics and economics as well.

In India, the Chemical Engineering course is the one that can make a possible career of a person in Chemical Engineering. The course of Chemical Engineering includes study about few or all of Organic Chemistry, Digital Electronics, Inorganic Chemical Technology, Chemical Reaction Engineering Lab, Microelectronics processing and Polymer Technology. It is a 4 year undergraduate degree program. Not all engineering institutions provide this course. Only a few institutions in provides this course.

Eligibility

The minimum age should be 17 years, 10+2 qualified with 50% in science stream and candidate needs to appear in the entrance exam, which is held in state and all India basis, CET, and AIEEE. To get admission in engineering merit will be calculated through your marks in the school-leaving exam and entrance exam marks.

To become a Chemical Engineer you should have B.E, B.Tech, or Postgraduate degree in Chemical Engineering. The three-year diploma course is also available after 10th Standard (SSC). By which one can also diploma after 10th standard and can become a direct second-year entrant for this course.

Scope

The career options are wide-ranging and different for Chemical Engineer. In India, many job opportunities are available in both public and private sectors. They can get the job in Chemical Plants, Petrochemical Plants, Pharmaceuticals, petroleum refining Plants, Mineral Based Industries, Electronics Industry, Photographic Equipment Units, Clothing Units, Pulp and Paper Manufacturing, Aircraft Industry etc.

A degree holder can get the job easily in any chemical firm or plant. They can work on chemical plants or industries as Supervisor, Technical Specialist, Project Manager, Project Engineer, Researcher, Quality Manager, Senior Process Engineer, Product Development Engineer, Fuel meter Calibration Technician and Environmental safety and Regulatory Manager.
