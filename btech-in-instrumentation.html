B. Tech Instrumentation Control Engineering

Course

Instrumentation Control Engineering is the course that deals with handling heavy equipment. The work here is to maintain the working function of the equipment system.

An engineer of Instrumentation and control is also subjected to writing computer programs for the equipment. They are needed to work with the collaboration of internal staff, Operational Engineers, Design Engineers, and Purchasers. The responsibility is to manage the operations while presenting the finding and analyzing the data and present it in writings. The major work consists of testing, modifying and maintaining existing systems.

Eligibility

  • To get admission to Bachelor’s degree of Instrument Control Engineering is to pass 10+2 with science stream.
  • It is necessary to study English, Physics, Math with Chemistry or Biology or Biotechnology as optional subjects.
  • The minimum passing marks in the 12th standard have to be above 45% marks. The colleges are having their own entrance exam.
  • The duration of the course is for 4 years. The admission form can be filled in EDUPROV’s website.

Further study in Instrumentation Control Engineering is to go for 2 years, Master's Degree. The educational qualification needed is to pass a Bachelor of Instrumentation Control Engineering. To get avail in the course a candidate has to clear GATE or should qualify the entrance exam conducted by the college they want to enter.

Scope

For a fresher, they are liable to work as an assistant under the supervision of an expert. There are other job scopes like Instrumentation Engineer, Manage and Deputy Manager, Assistant Manager, Executive Trainee, Instrumentation Control Systems Engineer, Supervisor, Instrument Engineer, etc. A person of this degree caliber is also required to work in nuclear stations, Thermal Power Station and Instrument manufacturers and users.

There are international companies that are always looking forward to a skill full Instrumentation Control Engineer. A presentable resume is good enough to apply for jobs in multinational firms.
