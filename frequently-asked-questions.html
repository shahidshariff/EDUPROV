Bachelor of commerce in computer applications is a 3 year undergraduate course which combines the study of both commerce and computer technology in the field of money and finance. It is more about applying technology for the best growth in the economy. After the completion of this degree students can join their higher education such as MBA, MCA, and MCOM etc. Subjects in this course include accounting, programming languages, e-commerce, GST etc.

Whereas BBA computer applications is also a 3 year undergraduate degree that is dealing in the study of business administration in computer technology. This course will be very helpful for students to face the tough challenges in the IT industry and business development industry. Admissions to both the courses will be done either through entrance exam or through the candidate’s performance in their 12th standard.

What is the difference between Machine design and Mechanical Engineering?

What is the difference between Machine design and Mechanical Engineering?

Machine design is a 3 years course which covers the basic fundamentals of mechanical designs and topics such as shafts, gears, break failing theories etc. Machine design can also be studied as a specialization in mechanical engineering.

Whereas Mechanical engineering is a 4 year under graduate degree course which studies the design, development, construction and testing of mechanical devices in motors or machines.

What is Architectural Engineering?

What is Architectural Engineering?

Architectural Engineering is a 4 year undergraduate course which is also an engineering discipline dealing in the planning, construction, operations and building of any integrated structures. It also teaches the students to look into all the internal and external components such as electricity, plumbing, water sustainable lifestyle, lighting, fire protection. Architectural engineering is a mixed combination of art and building with engineering principles.

What is the difference between M.Pharm and D.Pharm?

What is the difference between M.Pharm and D.Pharm?

M.Pharm is a 2-year post graduate course which is followed after completion of B. Pharm by many students. The course is a pharmaceutical industry related which deals with the science and study of drugs and medicine. It involves both theoretical and practical knowledge in pharmacy and helps in developing Medication with the help of better chemicals and technology.

Whereas D.Pharm is a 2 year diploma course in pharmacy. This course deals with the study of pharmaceutical sciences and medical field to pursue a career in the long run. This course is apt for those students who want to work under a professional and licensed pharmacist in hospitals and clinical pharmacies. But after completion of their diploma students need to do their bachelors in order to do their masters in pharmacy. Students can write entrance exams for admission such as GPAT etc.

What is the Genetic Engineering and Biotechnology Engineering?

What is the Genetic Engineering and Biotechnology Engineering?

Genetic Engineering is a 4 year undergraduate course in the engineering stream which can be pursued in both B.Tech and B.E. genetic engineering is the art of using DNA technology to make an organism. Such as altering the DNA and manipulating one or more genes to create another species from it. Genetic engineering makes use of biotechnology engineering in the direct manipulation of organisms. Admissions for B.tech genetic engineering can be done through entrance exams or direct admissions.

Whereas Biotechnology engineering is also a very on demand course in the engineering stream which studies the micro- organisms, bio-organisms and the cell functioning in living beings. Bio technology is a mixed combination of applied biology and chemical engineering. It is 4 years under graduate course and admissions for this course are done through entrance exam or through direct admissions.

What is the difference between M.D and M.S?

What is the difference between M.D and M.S?

MD stands for Doctor of Medicine while MS stands for Master of Surgery. MD is non-surgical and doesn’t involve in conducting any kinds of surgeries. Whereas an MS involves surgical expertise and skills. There are various specializations that an MD or MS can choose after the completion of their MBBS degree. If you choose MS then you stand out to perform surgeries with your skills and knowledge with tools and scissors.

Whereas if you choose MD then you will work as a specialist to prescribe drugs and treatment to patients without having the involvement of tools and surgical equipment. However if needed an MD also can conduct a surgery if he is well versed in his field. The course duration is for a tenure of 3 years.
