B.Tech Motorsport Engineering

Course

Bachelor of Technology (B. Tech.) or Bachelor of Engineering (B.E.) in Motorsport is an undergraduate course for the duration of four years offered by an engineering college. From the name you may guess that it is some course, that deals with automobiles and may get confused with mechanical engineering too. Since both of them deal with motors. You are absolutely correct, it is indeed a blend of Automobile engineering and Mechanical engineering. Yeah, you will learn about them both. Motorsport engineers are fully dedicated to providing innovative solutions to the Motorsport industry. As you know the population of automobiles is increasing as humans and innovation in the automobile industry have been done daily. So the scope for this course is too high where the world is trying to reduce global warming with the help of innovation in the automobile industry.

A Motorsport engineer has core expertise in subjects like stress analysis, engineering dynamics, chassis engineering, automotive engines, vehicle crash engineering, racing engine design, vehicle aerodynamics. Some other basic subjects are dealt with in the course like mathematics, electrical engineering (basics), fluid mechanics, microprocessors, etc. After completing four years (including internship), one is conferred with the degree of B.Tech/B.E. in Motorsport.

Eligibility

  • To be eligible for the course, one must have qualified 10+2 with core science subjects (Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics) with at least 60% marks.
  • There are two major competitive exams held every year for admissions in IITs and NITs and other national level colleges.
  • They are JEE (Joint Entrance exam) for IIT and AIEEE (All India Engineering Entrance Exam) for NITs.
  • Individual state-level tests are also conducted in some states for the state colleges. There are very few colleges in India offering this branch.

Scope

The Motorsport sector is the major recruiter. A Motorsport Engineer will be given the role of a design engineer, crash analyst, calibration engineer, safety inspector, product development in-charge, R&D officer, engine designer, etc. A Motorsport Engineer can also work as a lecturer in the engineering college but to get on to higher levels one must procure the degree of M.E./M.Tech in Motorsport or go for a doctoral degree. Not only the Motorsport industry but the automobile industry also recruits a major part of Motorsport engineers As the racing industry is more in demand abroad so is the demand for motorsport engineers over there.
