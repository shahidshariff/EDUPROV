B.Tech Marine Engineering

Course

Marine engineering is the branch of engineering which includes mechanical, electrical, electronic and computer science engineering for designing, production, and maintenance of on and around water vehicles. Apart from theoretical knowledge, practical training is also provided which includes candidates to complete 6 months of sea service. This course includes 8 semesters and focuses on subjects like generators and control systems, naval architecture, prevention and legislation part of the shipping sector, Marine electrical technology, etc. with an onboard training and voyage assessment.

The duration, of course, is four years and for students who have a previous diploma degree the course duration reduces to three years. After completing the course, one is given a Class -4 Marine Engineering Certificate of Competency by the Ministry of Shipping, India.

Eligibility

To be eligible to do a B.E/B. Tech in Marine Engineering, the candidate must have done basic 10 + 2 science stream education with subjects of Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics whereas for latent entries, diploma in mechanical or marine engineering should be from a recognized institute.

Age limit to apply is between 17 to 25 years and the candidate should be physically fit according to marine standards with no night or color blindness and unaided 6/ 12 vision.

Scope

The shipping industry grants a rewarding career as it is a vital sector in growth and stability with the advancement of the economy globally. Marine engineers are required in large numbers and after this certification, they can join in government services, transportation companies or scientific services. With further training and clearing aptitude tests along with advanced courses and increased experience on the sea, one may rise to higher ranks of third, second, first engineers and chief engineer consequently. Serving for the country will be a dream for most of the citizens. Your dream can come true if you do this course. Serve for your nation as a naval officer with marine engineering.

One may also do M.B.A to procure higher executive jobs of management in the marine industry. Doctor of Jurisdiction (J.D) can also be earned on concentrating on marine engineering law. Due to more employment opportunities abroad, one may join in armed or merchant navy, transport services where more travel is required and scheduled sailing contracts.
