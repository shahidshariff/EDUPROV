MBA Facility Management

Course

Wherever we look around us today there are different structures meant to enhance human living. This improves the quality of human life. It ranges from a sport or recreational facility to pools and gyms. For this facility which is there to serve the humans maximally, they need to be managed by professionals. This a profession that helps in the proper functioning of built environments like schools, Malls, gyms, sports centers, recreational facilities, shopping centers, etc.. With the help of integration of multiple disciplines like technology, process, place, and people.

Facility Management Professional (FMP) designation is a knowledge-based credential that will help to accelerate an FM’s (facility manager) smooth landing on the course and that will show he understands perfectly the profession he’s into. Facility management is good for:

  • Facility managers who want to increase their knowledge base in specific topics in the facility management profession.
  • Individuals who are transitioning into the FM profession.
  • Other practitioners like architects and designers.
  • Service providers and facility-related products.
  • Fresh students who are starting from colleges, universities and technical programs.
  • FMs with experience pursuing CFM credentials.

Eligibility

  • Diploma
  • Undergraduate Degree
  • Postgraduate Degree
  • Postgraduate Diploma

One can opt for a part-time/full-time course as per their convenience. Each course has different eligibility criteria. One who has completed 10+2 is eligible for the undergraduate course (BBA). In the case of a Postgraduate degree, he/she should have at least 50% graduation marks from any recognized institution are eligible for a postgraduate degree, advanced certificate, and postgraduate diploma. Some universities conduct entrance examination for admission. Entrance examinations such as CAT, GMAT, GATE are most common for getting into the course. Institutions you choose to play a major role in the career.

Scope

After completing the course, a qualified student has a wide range of opportunities throughout the map, which includes, Assistant Property Manager, Building or Facility Manager, Construction Manager, Energy Manager, Environmental Services Manager, Maintenance Manager, Operations Manager, Project Manager, Safety or Security Manager. A facility manager can work in different sectors like hotels, hospitals, shopping malls, IT services, property management, engineering, shipping, utilities, water and sewage treatment, architecture and design, and more. One who chooses abroad opportunity earns more comparatively more than working in the local environment.
